Introduction to Ethics, Genetics, and Society

Offered Spring 2022 as a 3 week course (BIOS232). Coming Spring 2023: a 10 week course (GENE233).

Why trainees ideated, designed, and led this course

Human genetics research has an ethically fraught past, with a legacy of discrimination featuring racism, classism, ableism, and exploitation. At the same time, the last decade has seen the rapid and unprecedented development of technologies capable of mapping, modeling, and manipulating genomes. Despite this troubled history and uncertain future, most graduate training programs do not provide students with the tools to evaluate the broader impacts of research on society. Like many others, we believe it is essential to maintain a consistent, thoughtful dialogue about scientists’ responsibilities to mitigate potential social harms.

Course learning goals

1. Connect the historical context of genetics research to its modern-day practice
2. Evaluate the social and ethical implications of genetics research
3. Analyze how societal norms and structures, along with personal identities, biases, and responsibility, impact the conduct of scientific research
